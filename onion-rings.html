onion rings

prep time

30 mins

cook time

10 mins

amount of servings

6 servings

Tags : foods

instructions

step 1

Pour vegetable oil into a pot. Heat to 365 degrees. Meanwhile prepare onion rings.

step 2

In a shallow dish whisk together flour, salt, pepper, garlic powder, paprika and baking powder.

step 3

Dredge onion slices in flour mixture, shake off excess (I just do two at a time and tap together to remove extra flour back into dish). Transfer onion rings to a baking sheet, reserve remaining flour mixture.

step 4

In liquid measuring cup whisk together buttermilk and egg. Pour mixture into remaining flour mixture and whisk to blend (mixture should be similar to the consistency of pancake batter, if needed add a little more flour to thicken or buttermilk to thin).

step 5

Pour panko into a separate shallow dish.

step 6

Working with a few onion slices at a time, dip into batter mixture coating each side, shake excess batter back into dish, then transfer to panko and press and coat each side in panko.

step 7

Transfer to preheated oil and fry until golden brown (they should only take about 20 to 30 seconds per side). Fry no more than 10 at a time.

step 8

Drain onto paper towel lined baking dish. Repeat process until all of the onion rings have been used up.

ingredients

Large yellow onions

All-purpose flour

Salt and pepper

garic powder

Paprika

Baking powder

Buttermilk

Egg

Panko bread crumbs

Vegetable oil

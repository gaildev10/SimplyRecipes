southern fried chicken

A True Taste of the South passed down for Generations

prep time

15 mins

cook time

30 mins

Amount of servings

1 chicken

Tags : chicken

instructions

step 1

Prepare the coating:
In a large resealable plastic bag, combine the flour, paprika, salt, black pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, and cayenne pepper if desired. Add the chicken pieces to the bag and toss them gently to ensure every piece is evenly coated.

step 2

Chill the chicken:
Place the coated chicken on a wire rack set over a baking sheet, reserving the remaining flour mixture in the bag. Refrigerate for 45 minutes, allowing the flour to adhere and form a pasty texture.

step 3

Heat the oil:
While the chicken rests, pour about 1 ½ inches of vegetable oil into a deep fryer or heavy-bottomed pot. Heat the oil to 375°F (190°C).

step 4

Double-coat the chicken:
Return the chicken pieces to the flour mixture in the bag and shake once more, ensuring an extra layer of coating for a crispy, golden crust. Preheat your oven to 200°F (93°C) to keep cooked pieces warm.

step 5

Fry the chicken:
Carefully lower 2 to 3 pieces of chicken into the hot oil at a time, frying until golden brown, about 8 to 12 minutes per batch. Turn the pieces once during frying. Ensure the internal temperature near the bone reaches 165°F (74°C) using an instant-read thermometer.

step 6

Drain and keep warm:
Transfer the fried chicken to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil. Place the cooked pieces in the preheated oven to stay warm while frying the remaining chicken.

step 7

Serve with pride:
Arrange the crispy, golden pieces of Southern fried chicken on a platter and serve hot. This timeless recipe is sure to delight any gathering with its rich flavor and comforting charm.

step 8

Garnish with lime slices and serve.

ingredients

1 cup all-purpose flour

2 teaspoons paprika

1 teaspoon salt

1 teaspoon ground black pepper

1 teaspoon garlic powder

1 teaspoon onion powder

½ teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)

1 (3-pound) whole chicken, cut into pieces

Vegetable oil for frying
